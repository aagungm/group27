Problems

  • Due to the anonymity that the internet allows, it is much easier for an individual to change their identity online.
  • As people can reveal how much or little they want on social media, they have the ability to manipulate their profiles so that they reflect who they want to be, instead of whom they actually are.
  • People can share everything on social media including photos, videos and links. However, the ability to protect individual's online identity still very low.
  • People do not really care or maybe they don’t know they have already shared their personal details and important information to some of the company when they are watching some interesting posts in the external link from social media.
  • Some people do not feel comfortable when they want to share their identity for several group of people.

First Solution

LinkedIn

To avoid false representation on what is acknowledged as a professional networking site, our company aims to implement a feature that ensures the information regarding past employment and work experience is verified by the alleged employers.

Second Solution

Facebook

Our company aims to provide additional security to Facebook to protect individual’s online identity. To avoid external links that publish spam as information reflecting an individual’s identity to their profiles, and therefore having the ability to alter the presented identity of the individual, our company aims to implement a feature that notifies users of whether a link may be ‘unsafe’.

Third Solution

Twitter

To avoid the user will losing their privacy, our company aims to implement a feature that allow user to choose several group of people that can see their future post on social media.
